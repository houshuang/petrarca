# The End of the Beginning of AI

### How the transformer architecture changed everything, and what comes next

The story of AI in 2025 was defined by three things: the scaling debate, the agent moment, and the enterprise awakening. Each deserves examination.

## The Scaling Debate

There was a period last fall where it seemed like everyone agreed that scaling had hit a wall. The "Scaling Laws" papers from OpenAI and others had shown consistent, predictable improvements from simply making models bigger and training them on more data. But then progress seemed to stall.

This was wrong, or more precisely, it was looking at the wrong dimension. What happened was that scaling shifted from pre-training compute to inference compute. This was the insight behind o1 and o3 — you could get dramatically better results by letting the model "think" longer at inference time.

> The real breakthrough wasn't bigger models, it was smarter inference.

This is analogous to what happened in chip design: Moore's Law "ended" in terms of clock speeds around 2005, but performance continued to improve through parallelism, specialization, and architectural innovation.

## The Agent Moment

If 2024 was about chatbots, 2025 was about agents. The shift from "AI as oracle" to "AI as worker" fundamentally changed the value proposition.

The key products:
- **Claude Code** — Anthropic's coding agent, arguably the most transformative developer tool since the IDE
- **Devin** — Cognition's fully autonomous developer
- **Computer Use** — Models that can operate GUIs directly
- **Custom GPTs + Actions** — OpenAI's approach to agent building

What makes agents different from chatbots is **agency** — the ability to take multi-step actions, recover from errors, and work towards goals over extended periods.

## The Enterprise Awakening

The most underreported story of 2025 was enterprise adoption. While media focused on consumer AI products, the real money was in enterprise deployments:

1. Every Fortune 500 company now has an "AI strategy"
2. McKinsey estimates $4.4 trillion in annual value from generative AI
3. The fastest-growing category is "AI-augmented professional services"

### What Comes Next

The implications are profound. We are at the end of the beginning — the technology works, the business models are emerging, and the infrastructure is being built. What remains is the hard work of integration and the inevitable surprises.
